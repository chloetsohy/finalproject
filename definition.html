Definition of Surveillance Capitalism

The term ‘Surveillance Capitalism’ was first comprehensively defined within ‘The Age of Surveillance Capitalism’, a work written by scholar Shoshana Zuboff. This phrase is interpreted as the practice of companies utilising their user’s personal data to predict future behaviours for profitable benefits. According to Zuboff, the profitable benefits, otherwise known as ‘free raw materials’, utilises advanced algorithmic computation to transform it into behavioural predictions. This concept widely portrays the transition of capitalism. With traditional capitalism marketing goods through respectful services, it has now evolved to surveilling, referring to the overlooking of human privacy in order to predict sales for products. Hence, this makes our digital traces or any interactions with electronic devices often at risk of being collected without consent, then being used to predict and influence consumer behaviour.
